Allow me to make a fool of myself and make a few predictions.

## React will continue to stagnate

The market leader[^1] is showing its age - virtual DOM and synthetic events have more drawbacks than benefits in modern browsers. Instead of simplifying, React is now more complex than ever before. [Hooks](https://react.dev/reference/react/hooks) look simple but are a beast under the hood. [React Server Components](https://react.dev/reference/rsc/server-components) don’t even try to look simple (to be fair, they do add a whole backend framework to React). Server-driven frontend feels both magical and hacky [and can get you hacked](https://www.kaspersky.com/blog/react4shell-vulnerability-cve-2025-55182/54915/). [React Compiler](https://react.dev/learn/react-compiler) will attempt to patch the cracks, but don’t bet on it to make things simpler.
Of course, React still has a lot going for it. The ecosystem is massive. JSX is pleasant to use. And, historically it got a lot right - this is the framework that called out the once-holy MVC pattern and won.

### TanStack is the new kingmaker

Libraries like ~~React~~ [TanStack Query](https://tanstack.com/query/latest) have already pulled 27k+ devs into their Discord. Through Query, they discover [TanStack Start](https://tanstack.com/start), other libs and ultimately even [SolidJS](https://www.solidjs.com) - which offers the React-like syntax devs love, but without the bloat, rendering quirks, and baggage.

### The Local-First revolution is coming

[Linear](https://linear.app) showed us the way: no skeletons, no loaders, just instant data. Why custom-code optimistic UI when an amazing lib can handle that and much more for you? Caveat? These sync engines are beasts to build. Keep an eye on [LocalFirst.fm](https://www.localfirst.fm/), [Zero](https://zero.rocicorp.dev/), and [TanStack DB](https://tanstack.com/db).

### Cross-platform: The "Write Once" Dream

Tools come and go but none dominates.

- **[Expo](https://expo.dev):** Current leader and innovator. It is single-handedly keeping React relevant for mobile.
- **[CapacitorJS](https://capacitorjs.com):** Tried, tested, evolving-is that good enough?
- **[Tauri](https://tauri.app):** Great for desktop, but its mobile story hasn’t delivered the bang I was hoping for in 2025.

### The State of the Rest

- **[Svelte](https://svelte.dev):** State of the art with [Vercel](https://vercel.com) backing. Its unique syntax puts some off, but it is deeply loved by those who use it.
- **[Vue](https://vuejs.org):** Innovating in a better direction than React (replacing VDOM with [Vapor](https://vueschool.io/articles/news/vue-js-2025-in-review-and-a-peek-into-2026/)), but is not there yet. It might struggle to get a second chance from the industry, just like the completely renewed [Angular](https://angular.love/angular-21-whats-new).
- **[Solid](https://www.solidjs.com):** The brave developer's choice. It looks like React, has a small footprint, runs fast like Svelte, and features amazing reactivity with Signals.
- **[Preact](https://preactjs.com):** The sensible choice that sadly seems to have lost its momentum.

### The Verdict for 2026?

**Local-first** will become the standard for serious apps. The "React Killers" (Svelte, Solid, Vue) will continue to fight for second place with no clear winner. Meanwhile, we are still waiting on a truly unified cross-platform tool that realizes the dream of Web + iOS + Android from one codebase without compromises.

[^1]: [State of JS survey](https://2024.stateofjs.com/en-US/libraries/front-end-frameworks/)
